Capabilities

DRAWING RESCUE

Old prints into working CAD

Plants running 60-year-old equipment often have exactly one copy of the drawing that keeps it alive — a faded blueline or a torn vellum in a flat file. I rebuild deteriorating legacy prints into accurate 3D models and fresh, revision-controlled drawings, so you can quote parts, plan maintenance, and stop worrying about the day the original becomes unreadable. Working from scans, photos, and field measurements of the part itself where the print falls short.

DFM

Design for manufacture

Sheet metal and composite parts designed around your equipment, materials, and processes — bend allowances, layup schedules, and tolerances your floor can actually hold.

AUTOMATION

CAD automation

SolidWorks macros and Python scripting that eliminate repetitive CAD work: batch drawing exports, flat-pattern checks, parametric part families driven from a spreadsheet.

TOOLING

Fixtures & tooling

Weld fixtures, assembly jigs, layup mandrels, and inspection aids designed to shorten setup time and make good parts repeatable.

DOCUMENTATION

Build packages

Complete, revision-controlled documentation — dimensioned drawings, flat patterns, BOMs, and STEP/DXF exports ready for quoting and the shop floor.

About

Eric Fisher is a mechanical designer based in San Antonio, Texas, serving fabrication shops and engineering teams along the I-35 corridor and remotely.

Fifteen years split between fabrication shops, OEM product teams, and heavy-industrial plants — including cement and aggregate operations running equipment that doesn't get to stop for a redesign. Work has ranged from sheet metal enclosures and conveyor systems to fixtures, tooling, and industrial piping and sanitary tubing.

Fast turnaround, straight answers, and drawings that account for what the shop floor can actually hold to tolerance.

Tools: SolidWorks · FreeCAD · Python · VBA
